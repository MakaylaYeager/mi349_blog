A Day in My Life at MSU

Author: Makayla Yeager • September 25, 2025

Life as a senior at Michigan State University can be both busy and exciting. I am studying Information Science with a concentration in Human-Centered Design, and I also have a minor in IT. My weekdays are usually packed with classes, group projects, and independent study sessions. Every morning I start by reviewing my planner and making sure I know which assignments are due. I drive to campus, attend lectures, and often spend time in the library between classes. In the afternoon, I either head home or work on projects in a quiet spot on campus. Time management is one of the most important skills I have learned, because balancing academic work, personal life, and part-time jobs can feel overwhelming without a plan. Evenings are usually for homework, dinner with family or friends, and a little time to relax. By creating routines and sticking to them, I have found that I can stay productive while still enjoying the college experience.

My Go-To Study Tools

Author: Makayla Yeager • September 25, 2025

When it comes to being a successful student, the tools you use every day can make a huge difference. For me, my laptop is the single most important tool. It’s where I write essays, build projects, check emails, and stay connected with professors and classmates. I rely on software like Microsoft Word, Excel, and Google Docs for writing and organizing data. Spotify playlists are another essential part of my study routine because the right background music helps me stay focused for longer periods of time. Coffee is also part of my study toolkit—I usually grab a cup before tackling long reading assignments. To keep everything organized, I use digital calendars and to-do list apps. These tools let me plan ahead, break assignments into smaller steps, and avoid last-minute stress. Another underrated tool is simply having a comfortable study space. Whether it is a quiet corner at the library or my desk at home, having a dedicated spot to focus helps me separate study time from relaxation. Combining these tools and strategies has helped me succeed in both school and work, and they are habits I will carry with me long after graduation.
